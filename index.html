About JJ-Trust

Born in 1991, Enwedo, Jah-Amaka Progress is an optimistic and confident educator with a strong practical background in the allied medical sciences. One of her strengths is in the development of the ideal pedagogy for deaf education. She loves selecting appropriate teaching materials, which makes her to be successful and self-confident in classroom presentation.

As an itinerant teacher, she provides a warm, supportive environment for developing academic, social, and emotional growth. As a result, she instills confidence, self-esteem, and an optimistic attitude in each student.

Naturally, she has a high level of patience and commitment in working with young people and adults. As an analytic and versatile thinker, she is effective at developing and implementing creative ideas.

Interests

Sign Language interpretation

Interpreters must process language with optimal speed and accuracy, and they must think about spoken language and sign language simultaneously. Interpreting is also about being able to support the educational goals and outcomes. My research interest is to figure out how best educational sign language interpreters should be trained, challenges they face, and how best to measure their effectiveness.

strategies for students who are deaf and hard of hearing

Research over time has revealed a number of instructional strategies to be applied to students who are deaf and hard of hearing. These include: bilingual/bicultural method, auditory/oral method and total communication. I intend to explore what are the best strategies to teach deaf students to read and comprehend English grammar.
